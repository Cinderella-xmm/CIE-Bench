**Step 2 – Determine Elements That Should Remain Unchanged**

**Thinking process:**  
We now identify what **cannot be changed** based on exclusion from the "allowed changes" list and logical necessity.

**Core recognition elements:**

The **silhouetted trees** are not merely decorative; they frame the scene and are explicitly mentioned as being intertwined with the creature’s limbs. This means their **presence, silhouette, and spatial relationship** to the mountain must be retained.

The **sky gradient** (deep blue to soft pink) is referenced as a source for the luminous veins’ color, so the **original sky tones must persist** to ensure visual harmony. Even if the veins are added, the **underlying sky must retain its original hue distribution**.

The **mist layer** below the mountain is a key atmospheric element and provides depth. It is not mentioned to be removed, and since the creature’s “mane” is described as misty, it likely replaces or enhances the existing mist rather than eliminates it. Thus, the **presence and position of the mist layer** should be preserved.

**Functional/logical association elements:**  
The **integration of tree roots with the creature’s limbs** implies a **spatial and structural continuity** between the trees and the creature. The trees must remain in their original positions and silhouettes to allow this connection. If the trees were moved or reshaped, the integration would lose logic.

The **foreground hilltop with greenery** is a distinct landform. Though not mentioned in the instructions, it is part of the original composition and lies just above the mist. Since the creature is built from the mountain, and the hilltop is separate, it must remain **visually distinct and unaltered** to preserve the scene’s spatial hierarchy.

**Non-modified element deduction:**  
All other elements not listed in the allowed changes—such as the **exact shape of the tree branches**, the **distribution of leaves**, the **depth of the mist**, and the **color gradient of the sky**—must be retained unless explicitly transformed. The **relative positioning** of all elements (trees in foreground, hill in mid-ground, mountain in background) must also be preserved to maintain the original perspective and composition.

**Preservation Targets List:**
- Silhouette and branching structure of the foreground trees
- Position and shape of the green hilltop above the mist
- Gradient of the sky from deep blue to soft pink/lavender
