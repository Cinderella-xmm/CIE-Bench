**Step 1 – Analyze Editing Instructions and Determine Allowed Changes**

Description of reference image:  
The subject is a vintage-style green and white van, viewed from an isometric perspective, driving across a flat desert landscape. The van has a rounded front with two large windshield windows, a cream-colored roof, and a wooden luggage rack on top. Its body is painted in a vibrant green with beige trim along the lower edge and around the windows. It features multiple rectangular side windows, round headlights, and a chrome bumper. The van is positioned diagonally, moving toward the bottom-left corner of the frame, leaving faint tire tracks behind it. The background consists of a pale yellow desert terrain dotted with stylized brown cacti of varying heights and simple silhouettes. Each cactus casts a soft shadow to the right, indicating a light source from the left. The sky is not depicted; the entire scene is rendered in a warm, minimalist style with smooth gradients and subtle shading. At the top center, the word "Road" is written in elegant cursive script, with "TRIP" in uppercase sans-serif font beneath it, flanked by two short horizontal lines. The overall aesthetic is retro, whimsical, and illustrative, with a consistent color palette dominated by earthy tones.

Thinking process:  
The editing instruction explicitly transforms the original static image into a **comic strip titled "Desert Adventure Chronicles"**, featuring the **van as an anthropomorphic character**. This implies that the van’s form can be modified to express emotion (e.g., tipping its roof like a hat), which alters its posture and function. Additionally, the **scene changes across three panels**:  
- Panel 1: Empty desert stretching far  
- Panel 2: Cactus morphs into a whimsical spout (water fountain)  
- Panel 3: Lush vegetation appears around an oasis  

These transformations indicate that the **background environment is not fixed**—it evolves dynamically. However, the **core identity of the van** must remain recognizable despite anthropomorphism. The instruction specifies **retaining the vibrant hues and style** of the original image, meaning color scheme, art style (isometric, flat illustration), and visual tone must persist.  

Allowed Changes List:  
- Van’s posture and expression (e.g., tipping roof like a hat)  
- Van’s facial features (anthropomorphic eyes/headlights, expressive behavior)  
- Background scenery per panel (desert → mirage → oasis)  
- Presence and transformation of cacti (into water spout)  
- Addition of text bubbles and speech content  
- Layout structure (3-panel comic format, aspect ratio 3:1)  
- Environmental elements such as vegetation, water, and shadows in later panels

**Step 2 – Determine Elements That Should Remain Unchanged**

Thinking process:  
Core recognition elements:  
The **van's distinctive shape and color scheme** are essential for identity retention. Despite being anthropomorphized, it must still be identifiable as the same vehicle from the original image. Key identifiers include:  
- The **rounded front end** with dual windshield windows  
- The **green body with cream roof** and beige trim  
- The **wooden luggage rack** on top  
- The **multiple side windows** in a row  
- The **chrome bumper and round headlights**  

These features define the van’s iconic retro design, especially reminiscent of classic VW buses. If these are altered beyond recognition (e.g., changed to a sedan or different proportions), the subject loses its identity.  

Functional/logical association elements:  
The **relationship between the van and the desert setting** is foundational. Although the environment changes, the **van remains the central actor**, and its interaction with the desert (driving through it) must be preserved logically. Even if the desert becomes lush, the van must still appear to be traversing it, maintaining continuity of motion and purpose.  

Non-modified element deduction:  
Although the cacti change form (e.g., one becomes a spout), their **original presence and silhouette** must be retained in at least one panel. The **color palette**—warm yellows, greens, browns—is specified to be preserved. The **isometric perspective** and **flat illustration style** are also required to maintain consistency with the original. Therefore, even if scenes evolve, the **visual language** must remain unchanged.  

Preservation Targets List:  
- The van’s green body with cream roof and beige trim  
- The wooden luggage rack on top  
- The rounded front with dual windshield windows  
- The chrome bumper and round headlights  
- The isometric perspective and flat illustration style  
- The warm color palette (greens, yellows, browns)  
- The presence of cacti in the desert (even if transformed)  
- The van’s role as the main character in motion
