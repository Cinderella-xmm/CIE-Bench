**Step 1 – Analyze Editing Instructions and Determine Allowed Changes**

**Description of reference image:**  
The subject is a tall, conical chocolate tower composed of multiple horizontal tiers, each supported by chocolate-like cone-shaped pillars. The structure resembles a stylized Christmas tree or architectural spire. Each tier features transparent circular platforms revealing golden, nut-filled chocolate droplet shapes suspended within the center. These droplets are teardrop-shaped and filled with various nuts (such as almonds, hazelnuts, pecans), coated in gold foil or metallic sheen. The outer edges of the tiers are adorned with small chocolate pinecone-like structures, some gilded with gold accents. At the very top sits a large, teardrop-shaped chocolate ornament filled with mixed nuts and golden elements. The base is wide and circular, featuring embossed text: “LA MAISON DU CHOCOLAT PARIS” in gold lettering on a dark brown band. The overall color palette is deep brown with rich gold highlights, conveying a luxurious and festive aesthetic. The background is plain white, emphasizing the object.

- *"With cutout windows revealing chocolate layers inside"* → The **transparent platforms** in the original may become **cutouts**, so transparency can change; however, **the internal chocolate layers must still be visible**, implying **retention of layered content**.
- *"Decorate with gold foiling, intricate nut embellishments, and a subtle sparkle effect"* → These are **new decorative additions**, so **gold foiling and sparkle effects** are allowed changes.
- *"Brand signature at the base, with the name 'ChocoConifer Delights'"* → The **brand name changes**, so the original "LA MAISON DU CHOCOLAT" must be replaced.
- *"Accompanied by elegant seasonal motifs in deep brown hues"* → New **seasonal motifs** (e.g., holly, snowflakes) may be added, which are not present originally.

Therefore:
- The **shape** (tree-like structure) is preserved but reinterpreted as a **package**.
- The **internal chocolate droplets and nut fillings** must remain visible (via cutouts).
- The **tiered structure** and **conical form** are likely retained for visual continuity.
- The **base** is modified to include new branding.
- **Gold foil and sparkle effects** are new, permitted enhancements.
- **Original brand name** is replaced — thus not retained.
- **Pinecone-like decorations** and **nut-filled droplets** are key components that may be retained or reimagined.

Allowed Changes List:

- Brand name on base: changed to “ChocoConifer Delights”

**Step 2 – Determine Elements That Should Remain Unchanged**

**Thinking process:**  
Core recognition elements:
- The **tiered conical structure** is iconic to the original design and essential for identity. Even if reimagined as a box, the **layered, pyramid-like form** must be preserved to maintain visual continuity.
- The **teardrop-shaped chocolate droplets** filled with nuts are central to the composition and represent the core product feature. Their **presence and placement** must be retained.
- The **chocolate pinecone-like supports** between tiers are unique design features that contribute to the festive, naturalistic theme. They are not generic but stylized, so their **form and position** should be maintained.
- The **internal arrangement of nuts** within the droplets is a key detail indicating product quality and variety. This **content visibility** must be preserved via cutouts or transparent sections.

Functional/logical association elements:
- The **vertical stacking of tiers** creates a structural logic: each level supports the one above. This **hierarchical layering** must be preserved for coherence.
- The **central alignment** of the droplets ensures visual balance and symmetry. Any deviation would break the intended elegance.

Non-modified element deduction:
- While the brand name changes, the **placement of text at the base** is consistent with the original, so the **position of branding** should be retained.
- The **color scheme** (deep brown and gold) is specified in the instruction as “deep brown hues,” so the **dominant color palette** must be preserved.
- The **use of nuts as filling** is critical to the product identity; even if decorated differently, **nuts must remain visible**.

Preservation Targets List:
- Tiered conical structure with horizontal platforms
- Teardrop-shaped chocolate droplets containing nuts
- Chocolate pinecone-like supports between tiers
- Internal visibility of nuts within droplets
- Central vertical alignment of droplets
- Position of branding at the base (though content changes)
- Deep brown and gold color scheme
- Symmetrical, balanced composition
