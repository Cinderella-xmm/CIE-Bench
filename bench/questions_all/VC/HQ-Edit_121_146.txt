**Step 1 – Analyze Editing Instructions and Determine Allowed Changes**

**Description of reference image:**  
The subject is an ornate teapot positioned centrally on a polished wooden surface, viewed from a slightly low angle to emphasize its three-dimensional form. The teapot has a rich, deep red body with a glossy finish, adorned with intricate silver embossed floral and vine motifs that cover the entire surface in symmetrical patterns. The lid is domed and topped with a small finial, also featuring silver detailing and a central red accent matching the body. The spout is elegantly curved, crafted from polished silver metal, while the handle is sculpted into a flowing, organic shape resembling a stylized dragon’s tail or leafy flourish, also made of silver. The base of the teapot features a wide, decorative silver band with detailed scrollwork. The background is a softly lit, textured brown gradient with warm highlights emanating from behind the teapot, creating a halo effect that enhances its prominence. The reflective wooden surface beneath the teapot mirrors its lower half, adding depth and realism.

**Thinking process:**  
The editing instruction specifies transforming the teapot into a **limited-edition luxury candle holder packaging**, which implies a complete functional reimagining of the object. However, it explicitly states:  
- "Retain the intricate floral motifs as embossed gold details" → indicates that the **floral design pattern must be preserved**, though material (silver → gold) and placement (on glass chamber) may change.  
- "Ensure a rich camel red hue for the glass" → color of the central chamber must remain red, but now applied to glass.  
- "Add a polished wooden lid that mimics the original teapot handle contours" → new lid added, but **contour of the original handle must be referenced**.  
- "Incorporate an elegant brand logo on the base" → base will have new element, but **original base structure must support this addition**.  
- "Replace background with a luxuriously patterned fabric" → background changes entirely.  

Thus, the **subject's core form (teapot shape)** is being transformed into a **candle holder**, so the overall silhouette may shift. But specific **design elements** are required to be retained:  
- Floral motifs (pattern, not necessarily material)  
- Red color (now on glass)  
- Handle contour (as inspiration for new lid)  
- Base structure (to host logo)  

Allowed Changes List:  
- Subject function: changed from teapot to candle holder  
- Material of floral motifs: silver → gold  
- Material of body: solid red → transparent glass with red tint  
- Lid: replaced with wooden lid based on handle contour  
- Background: replaced with patterned fabric  
- Addition of brand logo on base  
- Overall lighting and atmosphere: can be adjusted to suit new context

**Step 2 – Determine Elements That Should Remain Unchanged**

**Thinking process:**  
We now determine what **must remain unchanged** based on exclusion from the allowed changes and visual coherence logic:

1. **Core recognition elements**:  
   - The **intricate floral motifs** are explicitly stated to be retained ("retain the intricate floral motifs as embossed gold details"). Even if material changes, the **design pattern itself** (shape, arrangement, density) must remain identical. This is critical because these motifs define the ornamental identity of the piece. If lost, the transformation would lose its artistic continuity.  
   - The **rich red color** is mandated to persist in the glass chamber. Although the medium changes (from solid body to glass), the **hue must remain consistent**—specifically “camel red,” which is a key identifier of the original aesthetic.  
   - The **spout and handle shapes** are not directly mentioned for retention, but since the new lid is to “mimic the original teapot handle contours,” the **handle’s form must be preserved visually** as a reference point. Thus, the **contour of the handle** is a core structural feature that must remain recognizable.  
   - The **overall silhouette** of the teapot is implied to be retained as a base for the candle holder, even if internal function changes. Therefore, the **basic volumetric shape**—rounded body, elevated spout, arched handle—should be preserved.

2. **Functional/logical association elements**:  
   - The **base** must remain structurally intact because the instruction says to place a logo *on* it. This means the **original base’s form and position** must be preserved to serve as a foundation for branding.  
   - The **floral motif distribution** must follow the same spatial logic: covering the main body, wrapping around curves, and connecting at seams. This ensures logical continuity between original and modified versions.

3. **Non-modified element deduction**:  
   - The **texture of the silver embellishments** (though changing to gold) should retain their **embossed, raised appearance** and **detailed craftsmanship**. While material changes, the **relief quality and complexity** must remain.  
   - The **spout’s curvature** is not mentioned, but since it contributes to the teapot’s iconic form, and no alternative is specified, it should be preserved.  
   - The **lid’s finial** is not mentioned, but given the importance of symmetry and detail, and since the new lid is derived from the handle, the **original lid’s top structure** may be implied to inform design, but not necessarily preserved.

Preservation Targets List:  
- The intricate floral and vine motifs in their original pattern and spatial arrangement  
- The camel red coloration of the central body/glass chamber  
- The contour and shape of the teapot handle  
- The spout’s curved form  
- The base structure and its decorative silver band  
- The overall rounded, bulbous body shape of the teapot  
- The embossed texture of the metallic details (even if material changes)
