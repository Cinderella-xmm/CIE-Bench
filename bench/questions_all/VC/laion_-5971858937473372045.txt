- The central figure into a **regal noblewoman** in a **velvet gown with lacework and pearls**, carrying **dark roses**.

- The setting into an **elegant arched garden with classical statues and stone pathways**, evoking an **opulent, timeless ambiance**.

This indicates that:
- The **form, material, and style of clothing** are to be changed (from modern wedding attire to Renaissance-inspired garments).
- The **bouquets** are to be transformed (from white roses and greenery to dark roses and vintage arrangements).
- The **background environment** is to be replaced (from a natural outdoor setting with trees and railing to a classical garden with arches and statues).

However, the instruction does **not** specify any changes to:
- The **number of people** (three women remain).
- The **relative positioning** (central figure flanked by two others).
- The **posture and orientation** (all facing forward, standing upright).
- The **presence of bouquets** (they are still held, but their content changes).
- The **facial expressions** (laughing/smiling — though mood may be preserved stylistically).

Thus, elements related to **identity, count, spatial arrangement, and gesture** are implicitly retained unless contradicted.

**Step 2 – Determine Elements That Should Remain Unchanged**

**Thinking process:**  
We now determine what must be **retained** based on exclusion from the "allowed changes" list and core identity logic.

1. **Core recognition elements:**  
   - The **number of individuals (three)** is essential to maintain the group structure. Changing this would fundamentally alter the scene’s meaning.

   - The **act of holding bouquets** is a key action that remains in the instruction — only the bouquet type changes. Therefore, the **gesture of holding flowers in front** must be preserved.
   - The **facial expressions (smiling/laughing)** are implied in the original and likely intended to be preserved in tone, even if stylized. While not strictly required, the emotional state contributes to coherence.

2. **Functional/logical association elements:**  
   - The **positional relationship between the figures** (side-by-side, aligned horizontally) must remain intact to preserve the compositional logic of a group portrait.
   - The **hand positions** (holding bouquets) are functional — they anchor the objects in space and suggest interaction. Even if bouquet content changes, the hand pose should be consistent.

3. **Non-modified element deduction:**  
   - The **presence of all three women** is not mentioned as changeable, so it must be retained.
   - The **overall posture (standing upright, facing forward)** is not specified to change, so it should remain.
   - The **bouquet-holding gesture** is referenced in the instruction (they still hold flowers), so the physical act must persist.
   - The **general body shape and proportions** of the figures are not specified to be altered, so they should be preserved for consistency with human form.

Even though the clothes, flowers, and background are to be transformed, the **structural layout** of the subjects — their number, position, orientation, and basic actions — must remain unchanged to maintain visual coherence and avoid confusion about the scene’s intent.
