**Step 1 – Analyze Editing Instructions and Determine Allowed Changes**

**Description of reference image:**  
The subject is a large, luminous cosmic vortex dominating the sky, forming a spiral structure composed of swirling galaxies, nebulae, and star clusters in shades of pink, purple, blue, and white. The vortex appears to be three-dimensional, with concentric rings spiraling inward toward a central point of intense light. It floats above a dreamy forest landscape featuring tall trees with soft pink foliage resembling cherry blossoms. These trees are scattered across rolling hills near a calm reflective lake. The sky transitions from deep night at the top to a warm twilight glow near the horizon, with faint clouds and scattered stars visible. The overall atmosphere is ethereal and tranquil, blending celestial and terrestrial elements seamlessly.

Thinking process:  
The editing instruction states: *"Transform the cosmic vortex and dreamy forest landscape into a vibrant stained glass window; outline the spiral in bold leadwork, use rich jewel tones for the sky and trees, and add faceted glass textures to mimic light refraction, conveying ethereal tranquility."*  

Key transformation directives:
- **"Transform... into a vibrant stained glass window"**: This indicates a complete stylistic reimagining of both the cosmic vortex and the forest landscape as if rendered in stained glass.
- **"Outline the spiral in bold leadwork"**: The spiral shape must remain but now framed by dark lines mimicking lead cames used in stained glass.
- **"Use rich jewel tones for the sky and trees"**: Color palette will change to deep gem-like hues (e.g., sapphire, amethyst, emerald), but the *presence* of sky and trees must persist.
- **"Add faceted glass textures to mimic light refraction"**: Surface texture changes are permitted—glass-like facets and prismatic effects—but this does not alter underlying forms.
- **"Conveying ethereal tranquility"**: Aesthetic mood remains consistent, but this is an emotional goal, not a visual constraint.

From this analysis:
- The **form and position** of the spiral vortex can be altered in style (stained glass), but its **spiral shape and central placement** must remain.
- The **trees and landscape** are to be transformed into stained glass, so their **shapes and positions** may be stylized but should still occupy the same spatial roles.
- **Colors** are explicitly allowed to shift to jewel tones, so original coloration is not preserved.
- **Textures** (e.g., smooth bark, leaf detail) are replaced with faceted glass, so surface detail changes are allowed.
- **Overall composition** (vortex in sky, trees below, lake in foreground) must be retained for coherence.

Allowed Changes List:  
- Color scheme of sky, trees, and vortex (to jewel tones)  
- Texture of all elements (to faceted glass)  
- Surface rendering (to simulate light refraction through glass)  
- Addition of leadwork outlines around spiral and landscape elements  
- Stylistic interpretation of trees and terrain as stained glass panels

**Step 2 – Determine Elements That Should Remain Unchanged**

Thinking process:  
We now identify what **must not change**, based on exclusion from the allowed changes list and core identity logic.

**Core recognition elements:**  
- The **spiral shape of the cosmic vortex** is the dominant visual feature and central motif. Its spiral form is iconic and defines the image’s surreal nature. Even when stylized as stained glass, the spiral must retain its **circular, inward-coiling structure**. If lost, the image loses its defining character.
- The **presence of the forest with pink-blossomed trees** is essential. Though colors and textures may change, the **tree silhouettes and their arrangement along the horizon** must remain to preserve the scene's identity as a forested landscape beneath a cosmic phenomenon.
- The **position of the vortex in the sky**, centered and towering over the landscape, is critical for spatial logic. Changing its location would disrupt the balance between celestial and terrestrial realms.

**Functional/logical association elements:**  
- The **relationship between the vortex and the trees**—the vortex appearing above them—is key to the scene’s narrative. Reversing or removing this spatial hierarchy would break logical continuity.
- The **reflective lake in the foreground** is implied by the original image’s composition. While it may be stylized, its **presence as a horizontal reflective surface** must be retained to maintain environmental coherence.

**Non-modified element deduction:**  
- Although colors and textures are allowed to change, **specific structural features** such as the **number of spiral arms**, **relative size of the vortex compared to the trees**, and **placement of the trees in the foreground/midground** must remain unchanged to preserve visual consistency.

Preservation Targets List:  
- The spiral structure of the cosmic vortex (shape and orientation)  
- The presence and silhouette of the pink-blossomed trees in the forest  
- The relative positioning of the vortex above the trees  
- The presence of the reflective lake in the foreground  
- The overall layout: sky → vortex → trees → lake → distant mountains
