**Step 1 – Analyze Editing Instructions and Determine Allowed Changes**

**Description of reference image:**  
The subject is a long, straight asphalt road stretching into the distance under a stormy sky. The road is wet from rain, with visible reflections of light on its surface, particularly along the central yellow dividing line. Rain falls diagonally across the scene, creating streaks in the air. The road is flanked by grassy verges and low green trees, with metal guardrails on both sides. On the right side, a series of wooden utility poles with crossbars and overhead wires run parallel to the road. The sky is dominated by dark, heavy storm clouds, but a break in the clouds allows a soft golden light to illuminate part of the horizon, casting a warm glow that reflects on the wet road. The overall atmosphere is moody and atmospheric, with a strong sense of depth created by the converging lines of the road and diminishing perspective.

Thinking process:  
The editing instruction explicitly calls for a **surreal cubist transformation**, stating:  
- "Reimagine the rainy road as a surreal cubist tapestry" → this indicates a complete stylistic overhaul.  
- "Fragment and reassemble the scene into geometric shapes" → implies breaking down all forms into angular, abstract components.  
- "Emphasize angular road sections, overlapping quadrilateral clouds, stylized telegraph poles, and abstract triangular trees" → these are new visual constructs, not literal representations.  
- "With a palette of muted greens, grays, and deep yellows" → color scheme can be altered, though it must remain within the specified range.

From this, we infer that **all forms and structures** in the original image — including the road, trees, poles, clouds, and rain — are allowed to be **transformed into geometric abstractions**. The instruction does **not** specify retaining any specific object or form literally; instead, it instructs reassembly into a new artistic style.

Allowed Changes List:  
- Road shape and surface texture (to become angular, fragmented, geometric)  
- Trees (to become abstract triangular forms)  
- Clouds (to become overlapping quadrilaterals)  
- Telegraph poles (to become stylized, angular structures)  
- Rain (to become geometric streaks or patterns)  
- Lighting and color (within muted greens, grays, and deep yellows)  
- Perspective and spatial depth (can be distorted to suit cubist logic)  

Thus, **no element is explicitly required to remain unmodified in form** — only the *subject matter* (a rainy road scene) and possibly *some core identity cues* may need retention if they are essential to recognition.

**Step 2 – Determine Elements That Should Remain Unchanged**

Thinking process:  
Despite the transformative instruction, certain **core identity features** must be preserved to maintain coherence between the original and edited image. These are based on:

1. **Core recognition elements**:  
   - The **road** is the central subject. While its form may change, the presence of a **long linear path leading into the distance** must remain identifiable. Otherwise, the image loses its core theme.
   - The **rain** is a defining atmospheric condition. Even if rendered abstractly, the **presence of precipitation** should be evident through visual cues like diagonal streaks or droplet-like marks.
   - The **telegraph poles** are distinctive vertical structures with crossbars — their **functional form** (vertical pole + horizontal crossbar) should be retained in stylized form to preserve their identity as utility poles.
   - The **trees** are present on both sides, forming a natural border. Their **position and role as roadside vegetation** should be preserved even if abstracted.
   - The **sky and cloud structure** includes a dramatic contrast between dark storm clouds and a lit break — this **dramatic lighting contrast** should be maintained in tone and placement to preserve mood.

2. **Functional/logical association elements**:  
   - The **road's directionality** (leading toward the vanishing point) is essential to the composition’s logic. Even in cubism, a sense of forward movement should be implied.
   - The **relationship between poles and road** (parallel alignment) must be preserved to maintain environmental plausibility.
   - The **side-by-side positioning of guardrails and poles** on opposite sides of the road supports spatial logic and should be maintained.

3. **Non-modified element deduction**:  
   After excluding all elements allowed to change (forms, textures, colors), the following **must remain**:
   - The **presence of a road** as the central pathway
   - The **existence of rain**
   - The **presence of telegraph poles** with crossbars
   - The **presence of trees** on both sides
   - The **sky with contrasting cloud types** (dark vs. illuminated)
   - The **overall layout** of a two-lane road with guardrails and roadside vegetation

These are not about exact replication, but about **retaining key visual identifiers** so the edited image remains recognizable as a transformed version of the original scene.

Preservation Targets List:  
- Presence of a central road extending into the distance  
- Presence of rain depicted as falling elements  
- Presence of telegraph poles with horizontal crossbars  
- Presence of trees on both sides of the road  
- Presence of sky with dark storm clouds and a bright break in the horizon
